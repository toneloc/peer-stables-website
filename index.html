No tokens! No custodians!

We use of LN’s instant settlement capabilities to constantly rebalance channels to keep the amount of bitcoin stable in terms of an underlying asset.

At-will agreements; close the channel any time

These are at-will agreements, with no prospective responsibility for either party. This continuous settlement minimizes counterparty risk of a bilateral stablecoin agreement, until and unless a counterparty wished to close the channel and back out of the agreement.

Deep dive

Step by step, use Lighning to keep stable USD value.

The two participants are as follows: The stableReceiver wants to keep a set amount of bitcoin value stable in USD terms. The stableReceiver wants to be paid by a counterparty if the price of bitcoin goes down in USD terms (i.e. it will take more bitcoin to keep stable in USD terms).

How does it work?

The Stable Receiver is willing to pay a counterparty if the price of bitcoin goes up in USD terms (i.e. it will take less bitcoin to keep stable in USD terms) The Stable Provider is willing to provide that stability to the stableReceiver, most likely because he wants to be leveraged long bitcoin.

The stableProvider is willing to pay a counterparty if the price of bitcoin goes down against bitcoin (it will take more bitcoin to keep the stableReceiver stable in USD terms). The stableProvider is wants to be paid by a counterparty if the price of bitcoin goes up against bitcoin (it will take less bitcoin to keep the stableReceiver stable in USD terms)

A few notes on the trade-offs here before we proceed: The stableReceiver forfeits the upside of her bitcoin holdings. The stableProvider pays for any downside bitcoin risk. The stableProvider takes the upside of the stableReceiver’s bitcoin holdings

LN stables could accomplish this by settling price differences every minute. If a counterparty does not settle the difference after several attempts or, say, 0.5% price differential, the LN stable agreement is terminated.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to commonly asked questions

How much does it cost?

Peer Stables is open source and 100% free.

What information do you collect?

We do not collect any usage or analytics information when you view this website or use Peer Stables on your computer. While it would be really helpful to understand how you are using the app, your privacy is far more important.

What are the system requirements?
  • Peer Stables is actively tested on Core Lightning (CLN). We plan to add other Lightning spec implementations.
  • Core Lightning and Python are required to run the Peer Stables.
Can I use it on mainnet or testnet?

Peer Stables was built to work on mainnet and testnet, or try it out in a dev environment like Polar.

Which Lightning and Bitcoin nodes are supported?

The goal is to support the node implementations most commonly used by application developers building on Lightning. We will attempt to support more versions, but to date only Core Lightning is supported.

Can I contribute to the project?
  • If you use Peer Stables, then any feedback you have would be greatly appreciated.
  • If you want to stable USD value, you can use Peer Stables as a Stable Receiver.
  • If you want to go long BTC for cheap, you can use Peer Stables as a Stable Provider.

Still have a question?

Please head on over to the project's GitHub repo to let us know what's on your mind.
